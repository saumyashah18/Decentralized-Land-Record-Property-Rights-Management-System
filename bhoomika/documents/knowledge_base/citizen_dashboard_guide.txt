# BhoomiSetu Citizen Dashboard - Complete User Guide

## What is BhoomiSetu?

BhoomiSetu is a blockchain-powered land registry platform that brings transparency, security, and efficiency to property ownership in India. The platform uses a hybrid blockchain architecture combining Hyperledger Fabric (for authoritative governance) and Polygon Amoy (for public verification), ensuring both privacy and transparency.

---

## Getting Started

### How to Access BhoomiSetu

1. **Visit the Platform**: Navigate to the BhoomiSetu web application
2. **Login Options**:
   - **Phone Number + OTP**: Enter your mobile number to receive a one-time password
   - **Demo Account**: Use the "Demo Account" option to explore the platform without OTP
3. **First-Time Users**: You'll be prompted to complete e-KYC verification before making transactions

### User Roles

- **Citizen**: Property owners who can view, manage, and transfer their land parcels
- **Registrar**: Government officials who verify and approve property transactions (separate login via e-Pramaan)

## e-KYC Verification

Before you can transfer property ownership, you must complete identity verification. This is a 4-step process:

### Step 1: Aadhaar Authentication
- Enter your 12-digit Aadhaar number
- Consent to BhoomiSetu fetching your identity details from UIDAI
- Click "Get OTP" to receive a verification code on your Aadhaar-linked mobile

### Step 2: OTP Verification
- Enter the 6-digit OTP sent to your mobile
- The system verifies your identity with UIDAI
- Your Aadhaar is now linked to your BhoomiSetu account

### Step 3: DigiLocker Integration
- Connect your DigiLocker account to securely fetch PAN and other certificates
- This enables seamless document verification for property transactions
- Click "Connect DigiLocker" and authorize the connection

### Step 4: Completion
- A Soulbound Token (SBT) is minted to your wallet
- This non-transferable token proves your verified identity on the blockchain
- You can now perform property transfers and other transactions

**Important**: The e-KYC banner will appear on your dashboard until verification is complete.

## Transfer Process

### How to Transfer Property Ownership

1. **Navigate to Transfer**:
   - From Property Details, click "Transfer Ownership" in Quick Actions
   - Or click "Request Transfer" tab

2. **Verify Property Details**:
   - Confirm the property ID and address
   - Ensure you have completed e-KYC verification

3. **Enter ULPIN**:
   - Re-enter the property's ULPIN for verification
   - This prevents accidental transfers

4. **Upload Documents**:
   - Drag and drop or click to upload:
     - Sale Deed
     - Identity Proof
     - Any supporting legal documents
   - Multiple files supported

5. **Submit to Registrar**:
   - Review all information
   - Click "Submit to Registrar"
   - Your request is forwarded for manual verification

6. **Wait for Approval**:
   - You'll receive a confirmation that your request was submitted
   - The Registrar will verify all documents
   - You'll be notified once verification is complete
   - The transfer is recorded on the blockchain

**Important Notes**:
- All transfers require Registrar approval
- Ensure all uploaded documents are valid and legible
- The process typically takes 3-5 business days
- All actions are recorded on the immutable ledger

## Understanding ULPIN (Bhu-Aadhar)

### What is ULPIN?

ULPIN (Unique Land Parcel Identification Number), also known as Bhu-Aadhar, is a 14-digit unique identification number for every land parcel in India.

### How ULPIN is Generated

1. **Geographic Coordinates**: Based on your property's latitude and longitude
2. **Administrative Boundaries**: Incorporates state, district, and sub-district codes
3. **Parcel-Specific Data**: Unique identifier for the specific plot

### Why ULPIN Matters

- **Nationwide Compatibility**: Works across all states and territories
- **Prevents Duplication**: Each parcel has exactly one ULPIN
- **Enables Digital Transactions**: Foundation for blockchain-based property records
- **Interoperability**: Compatible with other government systems

### Where to Find Your ULPIN

- Displayed prominently on every property card
- Shown in Property Details view
- Included in all official documents
- Used for search and verification

## Blockchain Technology Explained

### Hybrid Architecture

BhoomiSetu uses two blockchain layers for optimal security and transparency:

**1. Hyperledger Fabric (Authoritative Layer)**:
- **Purpose**: Primary ledger for governance and property metadata
- **Privacy**: Permissioned network - only authorized registrars can commit data
- **Function**: Handles land transfers, ownership records, and complex transactions
- **Storage**: SHA256 hashes of all assets in the Fabric State Database

**2. Polygon Amoy (Public Verification Layer)**:
- **Purpose**: Immutable public proof
- **Transparency**: Anyone can verify records without seeing private data
- **Function**: Periodic "anchoring" of Fabric state hashes to public Ethereum network
- **Verification**: Compare local hash with public anchor to detect tampering

### How It Protects You

**Immutability**:
- Once recorded, data cannot be altered or deleted
- Historical records are permanently preserved
- Prevents backdating or fraudulent modifications

**Transparency**:
- All transactions are traceable
- Public verification without exposing private details
- Zero-knowledge trust model

**Security**:
- Cryptographic hashing ensures data integrity
- Multi-signature requirements for critical actions
- Distributed ledger prevents single point of failure

**Decentralization**:
- No single authority controls the entire system
- Reduces corruption and manipulation risks
- Government oversight with public accountability

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Account & Access

**Q: I didn't receive the OTP. What should I do?**
A: Use the "Demo Account" option to access the platform, or verify your phone number is correct and try again.

**Q: Can I use BhoomiSetu on mobile?**
A: Yes! The platform is fully responsive and works on smartphones, tablets, and desktops.

**Q: Is my data secure?**
A: Absolutely. All data is encrypted and stored on blockchain. Your personal information is protected by Aadhaar's security protocols.

### e-KYC Verification

**Q: Why do I need to complete e-KYC?**
A: e-KYC verification ensures that only verified property owners can transfer land, preventing fraud and unauthorized transactions.

**Q: What is a Soulbound Token?**
A: A non-transferable digital token minted to your wallet that proves your verified identity. It cannot be sold or transferred to others.

**Q: Can I skip DigiLocker integration?**
A: DigiLocker integration is optional but highly recommended for seamless document verification during property transfers.

### Property Management

**Q: How do I add a new property?**
A: New properties must be registered by authorized Registrars. Contact your local land registry office to initiate registration.

**Q: Can I update property details?**
A: Property details are immutable once recorded on the blockchain. For corrections, contact the Registrar with supporting documentation.

**Q: What if my property status shows "Pending"?**
A: Pending status means the property is awaiting verification or processing. Contact the Registrar for status updates.

### Transfers

**Q: How long does a transfer take?**
A: Typically 3-5 business days for Registrar verification. You'll be notified once approved.

**Q: Can I cancel a transfer request?**
A: Contact the Registrar immediately if you need to cancel. Once approved and recorded on blockchain, transfers are irreversible.

**Q: What documents do I need for transfer?**
A: Sale Deed, identity proof (Aadhaar), and any supporting legal documents. The Registrar may request additional documents.

### Blockchain & Technology

**Q: What is a transaction hash?**
A: A unique cryptographic identifier for each blockchain transaction. It serves as permanent proof that the transaction occurred.

**Q: Can blockchain records be hacked?**
A: Blockchain is extremely secure due to cryptographic hashing and distributed architecture. Altering records would require compromising the entire network, which is practically impossible.

**Q: How can I verify my property on the public blockchain?**
A: Visit the Polygon Amoy explorer and search for the transaction hash shown in your ownership timeline.

### Documents

**Q: Are digital documents legally valid?**
A: Yes. Documents verified and stored on BhoomiSetu are legally recognized as they're authenticated by government authorities.

**Q: Can I upload my own documents?**
A: You can upload documents during transfer requests. Official documents are added by Registrars after verification.

**Q: How do I get a certified copy?**
A: Click "Request Certified Copy" in the Documents section. The request will be forwarded to authorities.

## Security Best Practices

### Protect Your Account

1. **Never share your OTP** with anyone, including BhoomiSetu staff
2. **Keep your phone number updated** for security notifications
3. **Complete e-KYC** to enable advanced security features
4. **Review audit logs regularly** to detect unauthorized activity
5. **Use secure networks** when accessing BhoomiSetu (avoid public WiFi)

### Verify Before Transferring

1. **Double-check recipient details** before submitting transfer requests
2. **Verify ULPIN matches** your intended property
3. **Ensure all documents are authentic** before uploading
4. **Keep copies of all documents** for your records
5. **Contact Registrar if suspicious** activity is detected

### Data Privacy

- Your Aadhaar details are encrypted and never shared publicly
- Only authorized Registrars can access sensitive information
- Blockchain records contain hashes, not raw personal data

## Glossary of Terms

**Aadhaar**: 12-digit unique identity number issued by UIDAI to Indian residents

**Blockchain**: Distributed ledger technology that records transactions immutably

**Bhu-Aadhar**: Another name for ULPIN (land parcel identification)

**DigiLocker**: Government platform for storing and sharing digital documents

**e-KYC**: Electronic Know Your Customer - digital identity verification

**Encumbrance Certificate (EC)**: Document showing property is free from legal dues

**Hyperledger Fabric**: Enterprise blockchain framework for permissioned networks

**Immutable**: Cannot be changed or deleted once recorded

**Mutation**: Official transfer of property ownership in government records

**Polygon Amoy**: Ethereum testnet used for public blockchain verification

**Registrar**: Government official authorized to verify and record property transactions

**SHA256**: Cryptographic hash function used to secure blockchain data

**Smart Contract**: Self-executing code on blockchain that automates transactions

**Soulbound Token (SBT)**: Non-transferable digital token proving verified identity

**Transaction Hash**: Unique identifier for blockchain transactions

**UIDAI**: Unique Identification Authority of India (manages Aadhaar)

**ULPIN**: Unique Land Parcel Identification Number (14-digit property ID)

**Wallet**: Digital account for storing blockchain tokens and credentials
